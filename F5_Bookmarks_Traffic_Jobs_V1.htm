Procedure to disable the WUPOS traffic in a DC:

Initial state: Balanced – Serves traffic in both Reston & Chicago DCs in the normal state

Note: The traffic status of the pool members in LTM URLs should only be checked on the active LTMs & not on the standby LTMs.

Steps:

Traffic movement for WUPOS involves a series of steps to be performed on the GTM, Apache Load Balancer & LTM layers while disabling & enabling the traffic.

------------

Step -1: GTM suspension step – To block new incoming traffic to the DC

 Based on the DC in which the activity is planned or issue is observed, traffic has to be moved to the alternate DC for the above URLs in the GTM layer.

Ø  Reston – Disable Reston on all the GTM URLs

Ø  Chicago – Disable Chicago on all the GTM URLs

Link to the automated jobs in rundeck to move the traffic at the GTM layer is given below,

WP1:

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/d108aad3-b969-41b8-80e8-76120ffd047a

WP2:

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/72d29f46-c8e2-4de8-bffc-55d0e740cbba

----------

Step -2: Local LTM suspension step - To drain the traffic faster in the DC that is suspended at the GTM level

Initial state of the LTMs in both DCs before performing this step – Local pool members (current DC pool members) will be enabled (green circle) and Remote pool members (cross DC pool members) will be disabled (black circle) on both LTMs.

Ø  Reston LTM – Reston pool members will be enabled; Chicago pool members will be disabled

Ø  Chicago LTM - Chicago pool members will be enabled; Reston pool members will be disabled

Link to the automated job in rundeck to drain the traffic at the LTM layer is given below,

Reston suspension:

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/70696e64-8c1a-42e1-b40c-38daced8c428

Chicago suspension:

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/779ab97c-825e-468a-98cd-43708d38a4db

After running the above jobs, there will be changes only to the Local LTM (highlighted in yellow) and the status of the LTM will change as below,

Local LTM – Remote pool members (cross DC pool members) will be enabled (green circle) and Local pool members will be in disabled state (black circle)

Cross DC LTM – No changes will be made

In case of Reston suspension,

Ø  Reston LTM – Chicago pool members will be enabled; Reston pool members will be disabled – Changes will be made only here

Ø  Chicago LTM - Chicago pool members will be enabled; Reston pool members will be disabled – No changes will be made here and it will remain in initial state

In case of Chicago suspension,

Ø  Reston LTM – Reston pool members will be enabled; Chicago pool members will be disabled – No changes will be made here and it will remain in initial state

Ø  Chicago LTM - Reston pool members will be enabled; Chicago pool members will be disabled – Changes will be made only here

Note: Both DC LTMs will be in the exact same status post this step on all URLs.

----------

Step -3: Draining the Silos on the Apache Load Balancer Layer to drain the traffic faster – This step is only applicable for WP1 URLs & not WP2 URLs

The changes in this step will be done on the APLB URLs & not on the F5 layer.

Initial state of the Silos before performing this step – ‘Init ok’

Link to the automated job in rundeck to ‘drain’ the WP1 Silos on the APLB layer is given below,

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/bb4fc1fa-a6f9-4000-92ec-87207c211ff7

After running the above job, the status of the Silos in the APLB URLs will change to ‘Init drn ok’

----------

Step -4: Waiting for the traffic to drain

For WP2, the draining time is a standard time. Wait for 30 minutes before moving on to Step 6 and that will be the final step for the WP2 URLs as part of their traffic suspension activity.

For WP1, wait for the DB session counts to come below ‘15’ or the session counts in Dynatrace to come below ‘600’.

Once the user session counts come below the required threshold, move on to Step 5.

----------

Step -5: Setting MBEAN (Only for WP1) – This step is to display a ‘system down’ warning alert (maintenance page) for the remaining users so that they refresh their browsers and start connecting to the Active DC

Link to the automated job in rundeck to set MBEAN on the WP1 Silos is given below,

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/840b3fe6-5ac7-473d-9f69-02dec62c22d9

You need to select the ‘Disable’ option to set MBEAN

The job to check if MBEAN is set correctly on the Silos (value changes from ‘false’ to ‘true’) after running the previous job is given below,

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/0ab9bc0e-a64f-4cf2-9055-17dd93d965f9

After running the ‘MBEAN check’ job, the output message in the rundeck log would read ‘Maintenance page is set successfully for <Silo name>’.

After 5 mins of execution of this job, move on to Step 6.

----------

Step -6: Performed again on F5 layer - Forcing the pool members offline in both DC LTMs – This step is to completely flush out the users and make the DC completely passive

Initial state of the LTMs in both DCs before performing this step – As of Step 2

Link to the automated jobs in rundeck to force-offline the traffic at the LTM layer is given below,

Reston suspension:

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/6af06b91-6058-4696-8afc-06de4ea58864

Chicago suspension:

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/9bfad125-3de4-402d-8e43-efd609e70569

After running the above jobs, there will be changes to both DC LTMs (highlighted in yellow) and the status of the LTMs will change as below,

Local LTM – Remote pool members (cross DC pool members) will be enabled (green circle) and Local pool members (current DC pool members) will be in forced-offline state (black diamond)

Cross DC LTM – Local pool members (current DC pool members) will be enabled (green circle) and Remote pool members (cross DC pool members) will be in forced-offline state (black diamond)

In case of Reston suspension,

Ø  Reston LTM – Chicago pool members will be enabled; Reston pool members will be forced-offline

Ø  Chicago LTM - Chicago pool members will be enabled; Reston pool members will be forced-offline

In case of Chicago suspension,

Ø  Reston LTM – Reston pool members will be enabled; Chicago pool members will be forced-offline

Ø  Chicago LTM - Reston pool members will be enabled; Chicago pool members will be forced-offline

Note: Both DC LTMs will be in the exact same status post this step on all URLs.

----------

Step-7 – Disabling the Silos on the APLB layer as a final step to completely shut the traffic in a DC

The changes in this step will be done on the APLB URLs & not on the F5 layer.

Initial state of the Silos before performing this step – ‘Init drn ok’

Link to the automated job in rundeck to ‘drain-disable’ the WP1 Silos on the APLB layer is given below,

http://wuinfradeployment.wuintranet.net/project/WUPOS-PROD-NEWDC/job/show/bb4fc1fa-a6f9-4000-92ec-87207c211ff7

After running the above job, the status of the Silos in the APLB URLs will change to ‘Init drn dis’

After Step -7, we’ll see that the user session count in all WP1 Silos will go to ‘0’.

After Step -6, the traffic suspension will be complete for the WP2 URLs.

Suspension of WUPOS traffic in a DC is complete with this step.

Step -7 – Manually re-balance the traffic if needed

Sometimes, if the WUPOS 1.0 traffic is in one DC for a few hours, it will take some time for the passive DC to start sharing the load. So, to re-balance the amount of traffic/load on both DCs quickly, we disable the Active DC for few minutes on the GTM layer for the below two WP1 URLs alone and then again enable them back. It can be done using the same GTM jobs mentioned in Step 1.

wupos.westernunion.com

wupos.westernunion.net
